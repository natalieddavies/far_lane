FAR Lane Analyst Recruitment Assessment - PART 1

Assessment contains data on the age of the resident population in each Western Australian Local Government at the 2016 Census.

Develop a model based on this dataset to provide meaningful insights as to challenges that may be being experienced by Local Governments in Western Australia. At a minimum your model must:

  • Contain two visualisations i.e. graphs etc
  • Contain three key metrics for understanding age and age related challenges
  • Enable comparison between Local Governments
  • Demonstrate your knowledge of best practice for model design

Western Australia's elderly are living longer which is putting pressure on the local governments to ensure adequate services and proper funding. Seven individuals were aged 104 at the time of the 2016 Census. Additionally, 162 Western Australians were over 100 years of age. According to the Parliament of Australia, a person is considered elderly after the age of 65.

The hypothesis being tested in this analysis is "There is a direct relationship between the lifespan of elderly and the quality of local services available."

This graph displays the amount of people over 65 years of age per local government area. The purple/blue colours indicate lower counts of elderly people, while the yellow/ red colours indicate higher. It appears that elderly people are more likely to reside by the coast and less likely to reside inland.

Pink Bars: Funding from Aus Government | Stars: number of aged service companies

There is a direct relationship between the amount funded from the government for the elderly and the number of service companies available.

Bentley, which is apart of the City of Canning and Town of Victoria Park, was funded $49.6 million and has a total of 12 different aged care services available. According to the graph displaying local government areas, Bentley is included in the Canning LGA. Canning had 11,810 people over the age of 65 at the time of the 2016 Census. This works out to be $4,204 if each person over 65 were to evenly receive funding. According to the Australian Institude of Health and Welfare, 68% of the elderly population receive some form of health care. 68% of Cannings elderly population amounts to 8030.8, which works out to be $6182.38 per person (if spread evenly).

Local government areas with least amount of people over 65 are in the more central areas of Western Australia. The highest amounts of people over the age of 65 is recorded mostly in the Perth Metro area.

These graphs correspond with the above maps. The first graph shows a very low level of funding for the LGA's that appeared on the map. Not all of the LGA's could be found. The dataset used for the analysis of the aged care services only included data of existing service companies. It could be assumed that only 4 of the 20 LGA's showed up because the other LGA's do not have any services. This could explain why these areas feature such low counts of elderly people. Possiblilies for this assumption could be due to a mix of elderly relocating to locations offering better support or due to a lower life expectancy in these areas due to a lack of support.

Interestingly, the LGA's with the highest amounts of over 65's do not match up to the graph displaying the LGA's with the highest government funding. Rockingham has an over 65's population of 15,405, with an annual funding of just under $20 million but only 6 age care service companies reported.

In otherwords, Rockingham has 3,595 more people than Canning, receives over $20.5 million less and has half the number of aged care services.
